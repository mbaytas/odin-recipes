Spaghetti Carbonara

Description

Spaghetti Carbonara is a classic Italian pasta dish that combines the richness of eggs and cheese with the salty crispness of pancetta or bacon. This simple yet luxurious meal is perfect for a quick weeknight dinner or an impressive date night feast.

Originating from Rome, this dish has become a beloved staple in Italian cuisine worldwide. Our recipe stays true to the traditional preparation, resulting in a creamy, indulgent pasta that's sure to satisfy.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound spaghetti
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup grated Pecorino Romano cheese
  • 1 cup grated Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese
  • 8 ounces pancetta or bacon, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Salt for pasta water

Steps

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook spaghetti according to package instructions until al dente.
  2. While the pasta is cooking, whisk together eggs and both cheeses in a large bowl. Set aside.
  3. In a large skillet, cook pancetta over medium heat until crispy, about 5-7 minutes.
  4. Add minced garlic to the skillet and cook for another minute until fragrant.
  5. When the pasta is done, reserve 1 cup of pasta cooking water, then drain the pasta.
  6. Add the hot pasta to the skillet with pancetta and garlic. Toss to combine.
  7. Remove the skillet from heat and quickly stir in the egg and cheese mixture, tossing constantly to create a creamy sauce. Add pasta water as needed to reach desired consistency.
  8. Season generously with freshly ground black pepper.
  9. Serve immediately, garnished with additional grated cheese if desired.
